If you're planning your first trip to China from New Zealand, you can now enter mainland China visa-free for up to 30 days. This exciting development simplifies travel planning and opens up a world of exploration and cultural discovery. Here's how you can make the most out of your first journey to the Middle Kingdom.</p> <h2>Visa-Free Travel: What You Need to Know</h2> <p>As of now, New Zealand ordinary passport holders do not need a visa to enter mainland China, thanks to a bilateral visa-free arrangement in effect until 31 December 2026. This policy allows you to stay for up to 30 days per visit, whether your purpose is tourism, transit, business, or visiting relatives.</p> <h3>Documents Required for Entry</h3> <p>Even though a visa is not required, ensure you have the following documents ready:</p> <ul> <li><strong>Passport:</strong> Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned travel dates.</li> <li><strong>Return or onward ticket:</strong> Proof that you will leave China within the 30-day limit.</li> <li><strong>Hotel accommodation proof:</strong> Booking confirmation for at least your first few nights.</li> <li><strong>Sufficient funds:</strong> Be prepared to show evidence of travel funds if asked by customs officers.</li> </ul> <p>Note that diplomatic, official, or service passports are subject to different rules, and travel to regions like Hong Kong, Macau, and Tibet requires additional permits or arrangements.</p> <h2>When to Visit China</h2> <p>The best time to visit China varies depending on the regions you plan to explore. Generally, spring (April to May) and autumn (September to October) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. These seasons are perfect for exploring China's iconic sites, from the Great Wall to the Terracotta Warriors.</p> <h2>Must-See Destinations for First-Time Visitors</h2> <p>China is vast, with diverse landscapes and cultural treasures. For first-time visitors, here are some top destinations to consider:</p> <ul> <li><strong>Beijing:</strong> Home to historical landmarks like the Forbidden City and the Great Wall.</li> <li><strong>Xi'an:</strong> Famous for the Terracotta Warriors and rich history.</li> <li><strong>Shanghai:</strong> A vibrant city blending modernity with traditional charm.</li> <li><strong>Guilin:</strong> Known for its stunning karst landscapes and the Li River.</li> <li><strong>Chengdu:</strong> The place to see adorable giant pandas and enjoy spicy Sichuan cuisine.</li> </ul> <h2>Why Choose CTS Tours NZ for Your China Adventure</h2> <p>Planning a trip to China can be daunting, but with CTS Tours NZ, you're in expert hands.
