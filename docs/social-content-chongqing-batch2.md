### FB-06 — Chengdu: The Most Liveable City in China

**目标受众：** 喜欢文化深度、慢旅行、非典型景点的旅客

---

Chengdu has been voted China's most liveable city for years running. If you spend a few days there, you'll understand why.

It's not about the landmarks (though the panda base is extraordinary). It's about the pace.

Locals in Chengdu genuinely don't seem to be in a hurry. The teahouses in People's Park fill up by 9am with people who have no plans to leave until dusk. The mahjong tables in the park are occupied by people playing for small stakes with the focus of professional gamblers. An ear-cleaning practitioner moves slowly from chair to chair — long metal tools, gentle technique — and their clients look like they've entered a meditative state.

The food culture is built around sitting. Hot pot is a two-hour event. A proper Sichuan meal involves multiple dishes arriving over time, each shared, each discussed.

Chengdu is the counterpoint to Chongqing's dramatic energy. Both belong in the same trip.
