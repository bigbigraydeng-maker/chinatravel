**Photo direction:** 地面仰拍，列车从楼层间穿出的瞬间。最佳时间：任何时段均可。

**Caption (English):**
There's a train that goes through the middle of a building in Chongqing.

Not under it. Not next to it. *Through* it.

Floors 6 to 8 of a 19-storey residential block. Built simultaneously with the station in 2004. Residents above barely hear it — rubber tyres.

This is the moment every person who visits Chongqing tries to photograph. It never gets less surreal.

### Post 2 — Hongyadong（洪崖洞夜景）
**Photo direction:** 嘉陵江对岸全景拍摄，夜景打光全开（晚 8 点后）。竖版构图效果最佳。

**Caption (English):**
No filter. No edit. Just Chongqing at night.

Hongyadong is an 11-storey building built directly into a cliff face above the Jialing River. Every night it lights up in red and gold lanterns. People compare it to the bathhouse in *Spirited Away*.

They're not wrong.

Free to enter. Best view from across the river, after 8pm.

### Post 4 — Matchmaking Corner（相亲角）
**Photo direction:** 人民公园相亲角，父母展示手写资料的场景。街头纪实风格，自然抓拍。

**Caption (English):**
Every weekend in Chengdu, hundreds of parents gather in People's Park.

They bring handwritten profiles of their unmarried adult children: age, height, income, apartment ownership status, zodiac sign. They display them on the ground or on small stands and wait.

This is the Matchmaking Corner. Parents finding partners for their kids — openly, methodically, with complete dedication.

It is funny and touching and uniquely Chinese all at once.

📍 People's Park (人民公园), Chengdu  
Free to visit. Busiest on weekends.

### Post 5 — Hot Pot（重庆火锅）
**Photo direction:** 鸳鸯锅特写，红汤沸腾，食材摆盘。复古霓虹灯馆子环境更佳。

**Caption (English):**
Chongqing hot pot is not the same as the hot pot you've had before.

The base is beef tallow, dried chillies, and Sichuan peppercorn. That last one doesn't just burn — it numbs. Your lips go tingly. Your whole mouth follows.

Order a split pot (half spicy, half mild) if your travel partner has a lower threshold. Order the sliced beef, the tripe, the tofu skin. Order more than you think you need.

This is one of the most memorable meals in China. We promise.

📍 Chongqing, Sichuan

### Facebook Post 2 — Why Chongqing (Educational / Trust-building)
**Tone:** Authoritative travel expert. Target: People in the research phase who haven't decided yet.

---

**Why everyone is suddenly talking about Chongqing — and what it actually looks like on the ground**

Chongqing has been trending on social media for the past two years. In 2025, Klook recorded a 12-fold surge in demand for the city. Southeast Asian tourists are booking flights at a rate 6x higher than the year before. CNBC ran a feature on it.

Here's why, in plain terms:

**1. The city looks like nothing else in China.**
Chongqing is built on limestone mountains above the confluence of two major rivers. Roads pass over rooftops. Bridges connect buildings at the 20th floor. A monorail station sits inside a residential block. The locals call it "8D Magic." The internet calls it cyberpunk. Both are accurate.

**2. It has a UNESCO site almost no Western tourist knows about.**
The Dazu Rock Carvings — 50,000 Buddhist sculptures carved into cliffsides over 400 years — were inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1999. Ask most NZ travellers and they've never heard of it. That's about to change.

**3. The food is genuinely different.**
Chongqing hot pot uses beef tallow, dried chillies, and face-numbing Sichuan peppercorn in a way no restaurant outside the region replicates properly. It's one of China's great culinary experiences.

**4. It pairs perfectly with Chengdu.**
90 minutes by bullet train. Giant pandas, teahouse culture, People's Park, Sichuan Opera. Two cities, two completely different personalities, one seamless journey.

The question isn't whether Chongqing is worth going to. The question is whether you get there before everyone else does.
