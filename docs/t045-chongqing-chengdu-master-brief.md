### Day 3 — Chongqing
**Meals:** Breakfast, Lunch  
**Accommodation:** Pagoda SASA Design Hotel or similar 4-star, Chongqing

**Morning — Huguang Guild Hall**  
Built in 1759 during the Qianlong Emperor's reign (Qing Dynasty), the Huguang Guild Hall is one of the best-preserved guild hall complexes in China. It covers 8,561 sqm on a terraced hillside beside the Yangtze River, with a height difference of nearly 40 metres. The complex served merchants from Hubei and Hunan provinces who came to Chongqing during the great "Huguang Migration to Sichuan" — a government-organised population movement that reshaped southwestern China's demographics. Ornately carved beams, painted pavilions, and traditional theatres are all open to visitors. Listed as a Major Historical and Cultural Site at the National Level (2001).  
*Content angle: contrast the ancient merchant world with the cyberpunk skyline visible just outside.*

**Afternoon — Liziba Station (李子坝站)**  
One of the most photographed railway stations in the world. Liziba is a straddle-type monorail station built into the 6th–8th floors of a 19-storey residential building in Yuzhong District. The building below is commercial space; above the station are apartments. Trains on Chongqing Rail Transit Line 2 pass directly through the middle of the building at regular intervals. Completed March 2004, opened for service June 2005. Visitors watch from the street-level viewing area or ride the train through the building themselves. The station was co-constructed with the building simultaneously — not retrofitted — due to Chongqing's extreme urban density and mountainous topography. It has low-noise rubber-tyred trains, so residents are minimally disturbed.  
*Content angle: this is the single most shareable moment of the tour. Every major travel media outlet has covered it. It is genuinely surreal and worth 30–60 minutes.*

**Late afternoon — Ciqikou Ancient Town (磁器口)**  
A well-preserved ancient commercial town dating to the Song Dynasty (960–1279 AD), Ciqikou sits at the confluence of the Jialing River and a small tributary. Known historically for its ceramics trade (ciqikou = "porcelain mouth"). The town retains its original Ming and Qing Dynasty street layout: narrow flagstone lanes, wooden teahouses, temples, and street food stalls. Famous for Mao blood curd (maoxuewang) and other Chongqing snacks. Free time for shopping and exploration.  
*Content angle: the sensory contrast with Liziba Station — ancient vs. futuristic — within the same afternoon.*

---

### Day 4 — Chongqing
**Meals:** Breakfast, Lunch  
**Accommodation:** Pagoda SASA Design Hotel or similar 4-star, Chongqing

**Morning — Dazu Rock Carvings (大足石刻) — UNESCO World Heritage Site**  
Located approximately 100km northwest of Chongqing city centre (roughly 1.5 hours by road). The Dazu Rock Carvings are an exceptional series of Buddhist sculptures dating from the 9th to 13th centuries, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1999. The site contains 75 protected clusters with approximately 50,000 stone statues and over 100,000 Chinese characters of inscriptions. The carvings synthesise Buddhism, Taoism, and Confucianism in a way unique to this region and period. The main visitor sites are Baodingshan (large-scale narrative carvings, including a 31-metre reclining Buddha) and Beishan (earlier, more purely Buddhist, densely packed sculptures). The aesthetic quality and diversity of subject matter — secular and religious, narrative and symbolic — are outstanding by any global standard.  
*Content angle: UNESCO legitimacy + the scale of 50,000 sculptures is hard to visualise. Position as the "hidden gem" of the itinerary — most NZ travellers have never heard of it.*

**Evening — Hongyadong (洪崖洞)**  
The defining image of Chongqing. Hongyadong is an 11-storey stilted building complex (diaojiaolou style) built directly into the cliff face above the Jialing River. At night, it is lit with thousands of red and golden lights, creating a glowing, layered spectacle that rises from the riverbank like a scene from a Miyazaki film. The complex contains restaurants, bars, tea houses, and souvenir shops across multiple levels connected by stairs and lifts. Best experienced after dark — the full lighting effect is only visible once the sun goes down. The view from across the river (Qiansimen Bridge area) is the most photographed angle.  
*Content angle: this is the hero image of the tour. "The moment your clients realise they're not in the China they expected."*

### Day 5 — Chongqing (Free Day / Optional Experiences)
**Meals:** Breakfast  
**Accommodation:** Pagoda SASA Design Hotel or similar 4-star, Chongqing

A free day to explore Chongqing at your own pace — or join an optional excursion. Suggested local experiences:

**Yangtze River Cableway (长江索道)**  
One of Chongqing's most iconic and nostalgic experiences. The aerial tramway opened in 1987 and crosses directly above the Yangtze River, connecting Yuzhong Peninsula with Nanan District. The crossing takes about 4 minutes and gives an unmatched overhead view of the river and the city's dramatic vertical topography. One of the last urban cable car commuter systems remaining in any major Chinese city — a living piece of transport history.  
*Fare: small (a few RMB each way). Queues can be long on weekends — go early morning.*

**Jiefangbei Pedestrian Square (解放碑)**  
Chongqing's commercial and cultural heart. The Liberation Monument at the centre is an 27-metre clock tower built in 1945 to commemorate the end of the Second Sino-Japanese War. The surrounding pedestrian zone is packed with shopping malls, street food vendors, local restaurants, and the city's most concentrated nightlife. At night the area is fully illuminated and buzzing — good contrast to Hongyadong's atmospheric glow from Day 4.  
*Walking distance from most central hotels. Best experienced in the evening.*

**Yangtze & Jialing River Night Cruise (两江夜游)**  
A 45–60 minute evening boat cruise at the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. The cruise passes Hongyadong from the water (the most dramatic angle), under the illuminated bridges, and along the full neon skyline of Yuzhong Peninsula. One of the best ways to understand Chongqing's geography — the city literally surrounds both rivers on a peninsula of hills.  
*Widely available from the Chaotianmen Dock area. Best after 8pm when full city lighting is active.*

**Hot Pot Dinner (火锅)**  
Chongqing is the birthplace and undisputed capital of Chinese hot pot — significantly spicier and more complex than the Sichuan hot pot served elsewhere. The traditional Chongqing version uses a blend of dried chillies, Sichuan peppercorn (numbing rather than just hot), beef tallow, and doubanjiang fermented bean paste. The communal dining format is a cultural experience as much as a meal. Local recommendation: request a split pot (鸳鸯锅) if group spice tolerance varies.  
*Content angle: "This is the meal Chongqing is most famous for. It will change how you think about hot pot."*

### Day 7 — Chengdu
**Meals:** Breakfast, Lunch  
**Accommodation:** Holiday Inn Express Chengdu Gulou or similar 4-star, Chengdu

**Morning — Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding (成都大熊猫繁育研究基地)**  
World's leading giant panda conservation and research facility, established 1987. Home to more than 200 giant pandas and red pandas across 3,500 acres of bamboo forest habitats. Visitors can observe pandas at close range — particularly active in the morning when feeding. The base runs active breeding programmes and has achieved significant success in captive reproduction since the 1990s. Giant pandas remain vulnerable with approximately 1,800 in the wild (IUCN 2016). Morning visit is strongly recommended as pandas are most active before 10am.  
*Content angle: this is the single most emotionally resonant experience of the tour. Photos guaranteed. Position it as a privilege, not a zoo.*

**Mid-morning — People's Park (人民公园) / Matchmaking Corner**  
Chengdu's famous public park and social hub. The Matchmaking Corner (相亲角) is a genuinely unique Chinese cultural institution: on weekends, parents and grandparents of unmarried adult children gather with handwritten or printed notices describing their child's qualities — age, height, profession, education, income, housing status — and seek suitable partners on their behalf. The activity reflects the particular pressure on the "only child" generation to marry. The park also has traditional teahouses where locals spend hours playing mahjong, chatting, and having their ears cleaned by itinerant ear-cleaners. Perfectly captures Chengdu's reputation as China's most relaxed major city.  
*Content angle: unexpected, human, funny, touching. One of the most memorable "slice of local life" moments in any China itinerary.*

**Afternoon — Jinli Ancient Street (锦里古街) & Chunxi Road (春熙路)**  
Jinli is a reconstructed Qing Dynasty commercial street adjacent to the Wuhou Shrine. Famous for Sichuan snacks (rabbit heads, glutinous rice balls, three-gun noodles) and traditional handicrafts. Chunxi Road is Chengdu's main modern shopping district — luxury brands, local labels, Taikoo Li open-air mall, and the city's streetwear and café scene. Together they show both faces of modern Chengdu: the heritage version and the aspirational consumer city.

### Day 8 — Chengdu (Free Day / Optional Experiences)
**Meals:** Breakfast  
**Accommodation:** Holiday Inn Express Chengdu Gulou or similar 4-star, Chengdu

A second free day to explore Chengdu or venture further afield. Two strong optional directions — one is a full-day trip, one is an evening experience:

**Option A — Leshan Giant Buddha Day Trip (乐山大佛)** *(full day, ~2 hours each way)*  
A UNESCO World Heritage Site (inscribed jointly with Mt. Emei in 1996) located approximately 120km south of Chengdu. The Leshan Giant Buddha is the world's largest stone Buddha statue at 71 metres tall — carved directly into the red sandstone cliff face of Lingyun Mountain at the confluence of three rivers. Construction began in 713 AD under the Tang Dynasty and took 90 years to complete. Visitors can view it from above (cliff path with close-up face views) or from a boat on the river (the only way to see the full scale of the figure). The surrounding forested mountain has additional temples and pavilions.  
*Content angle: scale is impossible to convey in words — the Buddha's shoulders alone are wide enough to hold a basketball court. The boat view is the hero image.*  
*Practical note: best as a guided day trip; high-speed rail from Chengdu takes ~35 minutes to Leshan city, then local transport to site.*

**Option B — Sanxingdui Museum (三星堆博物馆)** *(half day, ~1.5 hours from Chengdu)*  
One of the most extraordinary archaeological discoveries of the 20th century, Sanxingdui is a Bronze Age civilisation (c. 3,000–5,000 years old) that existed entirely independently of the Yellow River cultures that dominate Chinese historical narratives. Its artefacts — towering bronze masks with protruding eyes, gold-foil tree sculptures, humanoid figures unlike anything in Chinese art — defy all prior assumptions about ancient Sichuan. The museum was substantially expanded in 2022 with a new wing housing recently excavated finds. Widely described as China's most surprising museum.  
*Content angle: "You thought you knew ancient China. You didn't know this existed." Perfect for travellers interested in history and archaeology.*

**Option C — Sichuan Opera Face-Changing Evening (变脸)** *(evening, 2–3 hours)*  
Sichuan Opera is a distinct performing art tradition combining acrobatics, puppetry, fire-breathing, and the signature "face-changing" (biànliǎn) technique: performers switch between elaborately painted silk masks in fractions of a second, with no visible mechanism. The technique is classified as a national intangible cultural heritage and the method of mask-switching is a closely guarded trade secret. Evening performances are widely available in Chengdu's teahouses and dedicated opera venues. Best combined with a pre-show Sichuan dinner.  
*Content angle: completely unique to Sichuan; visually spectacular; highly Instagrammable. The moment of face-change is too fast to catch with the naked eye — guests always try.*

## 12. Attraction Deep Dives (for content generation use)

*(Expanded facts beyond the itinerary — use for blog posts, social captions, SEO pages)*

### Liziba Station
- Line 2, Chongqing Rail Transit (straddle-type monorail)
- Floors 6–8 of a 19-storey mixed-use building (floors 1–5 commercial, 9–19 residential)
- Completed March 2004; opened June 18, 2005
- First through-building rail station in China
- Station and building constructed simultaneously ("station-bridge separation" structural method)
- Rubber-tyred trains significantly reduce noise for residents
- Viewing spot: street-level plaza below the building; best angles from across the road
- Can ride through the building: take Line 2 from any adjacent station

### Hongyadong
- 11-storey stilted structure (diaojiaolou) built into the cliff above the Jialing River
- Best viewed after dark from across the river (Qiansimen Bridge area)
- Contains restaurants, bars, tea houses, shops across interconnected levels
- Stairways, lifts, and walkways connect all floors
- "Ghibli effect" widely referenced in travel media — resembles the bathhouse in Spirited Away
- Free to enter the complex; individual restaurants/shops charge separately

### Dazu Rock Carvings
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (inscribed 1999)
- ~100km northwest of Chongqing city; ~1.5 hours by road
- 75 protected sites; ~50,000 statues; 100,000+ Chinese character inscriptions
- Main visitor sites: Baodingshan and Beishan
- Baodingshan: large-scale commissioned carvings (late 12th–13th century); 31-metre reclining Nirvana Buddha; narrative Buddhist scenes
- Beishan: earlier, more purely Buddhist carvings (9th–10th century); densely packed, high aesthetic quality
- Unique in synthesising Buddhism, Taoism, and Confucianism in a single artistic programme
- 4K digital film available at Tourist Centre for context

### Huguang Guild Hall
- Built 1759 (Qianlong reign, Qing Dynasty); current structure largely from 1846 reconstruction
- Covers 8,561 sqm on a terraced hillside above the Yangtze River
- Built to serve Hubei and Hunan merchants during the "Huguang Migration to Sichuan"
- Complex includes courtyards, meeting halls, theatres, gardens — a full civic/commercial ecosystem
- National Major Historical and Cultural Site (2001); restored 2003–2005
- Traditional Sichuan opera performances sometimes staged in the theatre

### Chengdu Panda Base
- Full name: Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding
- Established 1987 with 6 pandas rescued from the wild
- Now: 200+ giant and red pandas; 3,500 acres of bamboo habitat
- Active breeding programme; significant contributor to global population growth
- Best time: 8am–10am (feeding, most activity)
- UNESCO Man and Biosphere Reserve designation
- Also houses Sun Bears, Black-necked Cranes, and other endangered species

### People's Park Matchmaking Corner
- Located in central Chengdu's People's Park (人民公园), adjacent to the Qingyang District
- Active most days, busiest on weekends
- Parents and grandparents of unmarried adults (typically 25–40) display profile notices
- Notices include: age, height, weight, zodiac sign, education, job title, income bracket, property ownership
- Cultural context: only-child generation pressure; parents take an active role in matchmaking
- The park's traditional teahouses serve gaiwan tea; bamboo-chair relaxation is a Chengdu institution
- Ear-cleaning practitioners (掏耳朵) operate throughout the park — a distinctly Chengdu tradition
